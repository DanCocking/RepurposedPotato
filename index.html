Fighting Hunger One Potato at a Time

We are committed to a unified approach involving local communities, working to bring each other up.

Our solution focuses around repurposing unused foods from the hospitality industry as well as accepting donations to feed those who need it more.

  • Repurposing food.
  • Donating food.
  • Providing jobs to break the cycle of systematic inequlaity.

Firstly, we plan to set up a volunteering program, where community trucks provided by the local council of Murrin Bridge, will be used to deliver repurposed foods from our partnered local cafes and restaurants to the Indigenous community. Subsequently, we will raise funding and community support for our project. We also present the opportunity for our partner’s customers to donate canned foods which will be collected by volunteer drivers. Customers may also donate loose change here, where 100% of the proceeds will go towards buying canned goods to assist the Indigenous communities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Some questions we are often asked

  • How will you ensure food is healthy and sanitary?

    After careful consideration of the control over food quality, we have decided to implement a three-step process to ensure that food transported is healthy and in date. This process will require the hiring of a professional food and safety team

    1. The restaurant or cafe is inspected to ensure that the kitchen and backend is sanitary to be providing meals.
    2. The restaurant or cafe will be given a ‘Repurposed Potato’ certification to acknowledge their contribution to the ‘Repurposed Potato’ program, this is given after an inspection from a certified professional
    3. As a trusted food place, the driver who is transporting goods will be instructed on how to complete a food quality check. This will include checking the expiration date and overall perishability of the goods in the time required to transport the goods to the specific Indigenous community.

  • Why The Name Repurposed Potato?

    The name Repurposed Potato originated in an online Zoom call with our entire team. As our mission is to repurpose food to Indigienous communities in regional New South Wales, we figured that the word ‘repurposed’ was crucial in our branding. In terms of the choice of ‘potato’, we noted that potatoes were extremely versatile, essential in many diets as a form of carbohydrates, and could be eaten in a plethora of forms and cuisines. Hence, we decided that the name Repurposed Potato was both attractive to the general public and perfect to represent our mission as an organization. .

  • Where will you start and why?

    We chose to target the small Indigenous community of Murrin Bridge first, mainly due to their close proximity to Lake Cargelligo and Condobolin, where we would be supplying the majority of our repurposed foods from. This small Indigenous community of 86 people makes our solution feasible with minimal costs, and as our project grows, we plan to expand our scope to other larger Indigenous communities in regional NSW.
